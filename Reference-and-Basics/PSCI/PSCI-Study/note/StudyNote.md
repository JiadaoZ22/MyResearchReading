# Study Note: Post-Stroke Cognitive Impairment (PSCI)

Summary drawn from the Chinese expert consensus and related literature, with links to source articles. **Figures are in the original articles;** key figure descriptions are listed below with URLs.

---

## Background

Stroke is a leading cause of death and disability worldwide. In China, stroke ranks first among cardiovascular diseases in terms of prevalence and disease burden (GBD 2016). Beyond motor and sensory deficits, stroke frequently leads to **cognitive impairment**—a syndrome now formally recognized as **post-stroke cognitive impairment (PSCI)**. Understanding PSCI is essential for prognosis, rehabilitation planning, and long-term care. This note focuses on the Chinese expert consensus and contemporary evidence on PSCI definition, screening, diagnosis, and prediction, with emphasis on tools and workflows relevant to clinical practice and research.

---

## Introduction

**PSCI** refers to any cognitive syndrome that meets diagnostic criteria for cognitive impairment and that **occurs after** a stroke or transient ischemic attack (TIA). It encompasses a spectrum from mild cognitive impairment without dementia (**PSCIND**) to **post-stroke dementia (PSD)**. The causal link between the cerebrovascular event and cognitive decline is central to the definition and has implications for both clinical management and research (e.g. prediction models, treatment trials). Chinese guidelines (中国卒中学会; 卒中后认知障碍研究圆桌会议专家组) have standardized terminology, screening pathways, and evidence levels for PSCI, aligning with international literature. This document summarizes those elements and connects them to practical screening, assessment, and prediction, including the role of neuroimaging and future directions (e.g. multimodal MRI, parcellation, treatment effect evaluation).

---

## Current clinical scenario

In current practice, PSCI is often **under-recognized** in the acute and subacute phases. Many stroke survivors are discharged without formal cognitive screening; diagnosis is frequently made only when families report obvious memory or behavioral changes. Outpatient pathways vary: some centres use a **three-step process** (history → brief screening with MMSE/MoCA → full domain assessment and PSD/PSCIND classification), while others rely on ad hoc testing. **Timing** is important: definitive PSCI diagnosis is usually established **≥3 months** after stroke, but **acute-phase screening** (e.g. within 1–2 weeks) is recommended for **risk prediction** and early intervention. In parallel, **multimodal neuroimaging** (T1/T2, DTI, SWI, MRA) is increasingly used to characterise lesion location, white matter integrity, and vascular burden—all of which inform prognosis and may support prediction models (e.g. SIGNAL2, CHANGE, lesion–symptom mapping). Gaps remain: limited use of quick screening tools (e.g. AD-8, Mini-Cog) in busy wards, variable access to full neuropsychological batteries, and insufficient integration of imaging with cognitive outcomes in routine care. Improving early screening, standardising assessment timing, and linking imaging to cognitive trajectories are priorities in the current PSCI clinical scenario ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661); [cn-healthcare.com](https://www.cn-healthcare.com/articlewm/20220630/content-1392346.html)).

## 1. Definition and burden

**PSCI (卒中后认知障碍)** is defined as a syndrome meeting cognitive impairment diagnostic criteria that appears **after** a stroke (or TIA). The definition stresses the causal link between stroke and cognitive decline and the relevance for clinical management ([中国卒中学会 2017; 卒中后认知障碍研究圆桌会议专家组 2020](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

- **Severity:** PSCI is divided into **PSCIND** (卒中后认知障碍非痴呆) and **PSD** (卒中后痴呆).
- **Burden (GBD 2016):** China had ~5.51 million new strokes and ~1.79 million stroke deaths; stroke ranks first among cardiovascular diseases in prevalence. Stroke affects not only motor function but also cognition ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).
- **Epidemiology:** In 13 studies from 8 countries/regions, ~44% of patients had overall cognitive impairment at 2–6 months after stroke/TIA; 30–35% had impairment in five cognitive domains ([Lo JW et al., Neurology 2019](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).
- **Outcome:** Post-stroke cognitive impairment is associated with higher risk of death, disability, depression, and loss of independence ([Obaid M et al., Geriatrics 2020](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

## 2. Screening and diagnosis

### Three-step outpatient screening (consensus)

1. **History and exam** — Identify possible PSCI and high-risk patients.
2. **Screening** — Use **MMSE** and **MoCA**; if normal, perform full cognitive domain testing to detect single-domain impairment.
3. **Diagnosis** — Classify as PSD or PSCIND according to impact on daily function.

**Timing:** Definitive PSCI diagnosis is usually made **≥3 months** after stroke. Acute-phase screening is used for **risk prediction** and early intervention ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

### Quick cognitive screening (1–3 min)

| Tool | Type | Use |
|------|------|-----|
| **AD-8** | Qualitative (informant) | ≥2 items positive → possible cognitive impairment, need further assessment |
| **cog-12** | Semi-quantitative (0–4) | ≥6 points → possible impairment |
| **Mini-cog** | Quantitative | 3-word recall + clock drawing; failure → possible impairment |

([健康界 cn-healthcare.com](https://www.cn-healthcare.com/articlewm/20220630/content-1392346.html))

### Overall cognitive assessment

- **MoCA (Montreal Cognitive Assessment):** ~15 min, total 30 points. **&lt;22 points** suggests possible cognitive impairment (MoCA 8.1 Chinese version in use).
- **MMSE (Mini-Mental State Examination):** Orientation, memory, attention, language; total 30 points. **&lt;27 points** suggests possible cognitive impairment.

([cn-healthcare.com](https://www.cn-healthcare.com/articlewm/20220630/content-1392346.html))

### Multidomain assessment

Includes memory (e.g. AVLT, DS), executive/attention (Stroop, CTT, DMT), language (VFT, BNT), visuospatial (Rey figure), and mood/behavior (NPI, HAMD) ([cn-healthcare.com](https://www.cn-healthcare.com/articlewm/20220630/content-1392346.html)).

## 3. Predicting PSCI

### Acute-phase neuropsychological tools

- **MoCA** (1–2 weeks post-stroke): AUC for predicting PSCI at 6 months ~0.902; cutoff 21: sensitivity 91.4%, specificity 75.8%.
- **NINDS-CSN 5-minute test** (orientation, memory, verbal fluency): AUC ~0.74; cutoff 6–7 for predicting 3-month dementia: sensitivity 82%, specificity 67%. Suitable for bedside use and when dominant hand or visual field is impaired.

**Consensus:** Stroke patients should be screened in the acute phase; MoCA and NINDS-CSN 5-minute test within 1–2 weeks can help predict PSCI (**Class I, Level B**) ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

### Risk prediction models

- **SIGNAL2:** Built in mild stroke; factors include age, education, acute cortical infarction, white matter hyperintensity, old lacunes, atrophy, large artery stenosis. Score 0–5 → ~10% PSCI; 6–10 → ~53%; 11–15 → ~74%. Cutoff 7: sensitivity 82%, specificity 68%. Limitation: mild stroke only, many parameters.
- **CHANGE score:** For 3–6 months post-stroke; AUC 0.74–0.82. Score 0–4 → 7–23% PSCI; 5–9 → 25–58%; 10–14 → 67–82%. Can predict PSCI in subacute/chronic ischemic stroke survivors.

**Consensus:** SIGNAL2 and CHANGE may be useful for PSCI prediction (**Class IIa, Level B**) ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

### Key infarction location (Lancet subjournal 2021)

Voxel-based lesion–symptom mapping across 12 cohorts: **left frontotemporal, left thalamus, right parietal** infarcts were strongly associated with PSCI. A location-impact score allowed simple visual assessment and performed similarly to other prediction models ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

### Cardioembolic stroke (non-valvular AF)

**CHADS2** and **R2CHADS2** may help predict PSCI in patients with cardioembolic stroke; both correlate negatively with MMSE/MoCA. Evidence: **Class IIa, Level B** ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

### Biomarkers

PSCI-related biomarkers include oxidative damage, inflammation, metabolism, and genetics (e.g. **APOE-ε4** associated with higher 5-year dementia risk in stroke in Oxford Vascular Study). **Consensus:** Evidence for biomarkers in PSCI prediction is insufficient (**Class IIIa, Level B**) ([thepaper.cn](https://m.thepaper.cn/baijiahao_21074661)).

## 5. Project context: PSCI 2026 and Hebei People's Hospital (Update-1)

From the first meeting with Hebei People's Hospital ([Update-1](file:///media/zoujd4/Lenovo-8T/Zoujd_IMI/f0_Database/Brain/PSCI_2026/Reports/Update-1/Update-1.md)):

### What we have now

- **Cohort:** All current samples are **PSCI patients** (no healthy controls yet).
- **Labels:** The codes **H, D, S, M** in report filenames are **modality indicators**, not disease labels:  
  **H** = MR horizontal scanning, **D** = DTI, **S** = SWI, **M** = MRA.  
  They indicate which scan type(s) the report refers to. There are **no quantitative or categorical disease labels** in these codes.
- **Data:** Scan reports (e.g. 印象), multi-visit structure, and **Records_Scan.csv** linking subject, scan index, scan time, scan modal, 印象, and H/D/S/M modality flags.

### What we are going to do

- **Ground truth:** The hospital will provide **Cognition Evaluation Chart (医学量表)** as clinical diagnostic ground truth (e.g. MMSE/MoCA or similar). Note: objectivity can be limited (e.g. treatment vs no treatment, survival bias).
- **Samples:** They will provide more **negative (healthy) samples** and **blood samples** as extra information.
- **Research questions:**  
  1. Which scanning/contrasts **contribute more** to the disease?  
  2. How to evaluate **surgery/treatment effect**? (e.g. brain ROI changes and cognition before vs after.)  
  3. Can we extend **brain parcellation to more contrasts** for finer 核团 parcellation?
- **Technical note:** SWI and some other sequences are 2D with ~5 mm slice thickness, which may be suboptimal for current parcellation algorithms.

### Connection to this study note

- Once **医学量表** (e.g. MMSE, MoCA, or NINDS-CSN 5-minute) is available, it can be used as the **outcome** for PSCI vs no-PSCI or severity, aligning with consensus screening and prediction.
- **Prediction models** (SIGNAL2, CHANGE, key infarction location) and **multimodal MRI** (T1/T2, DTI, SWI, MRA) can support analyses of “which scans contribute” and “ROI vs cognition” before/after treatment, in line with the literature above.
