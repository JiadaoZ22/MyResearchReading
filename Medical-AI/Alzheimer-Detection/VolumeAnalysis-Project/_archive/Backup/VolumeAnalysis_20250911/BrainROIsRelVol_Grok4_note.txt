Data Collection Approach: Data were compiled from open-access publications and databases focusing on FreeSurfer-derived normative brain volumes in healthy adult populations. Key sources include the UK Biobank neuroimaging norms (large cohort, N>18,000, FreeSurfer v5.3/v6.x, providing age- and sex-stratified means/SDs for subcortical and ventricular volumes, adjusted for eTIV), multi-cohort normative volumetry studies (e.g., ENIGMA, ABCD), and specific papers on cortical regions (Desikan-Killiany atlas). Relative volumes are calculated as (ROI volume / eTIV), where eTIV is estimated total intracranial volume. For aggregated lobes (Temporal, Parietal), volumes are summed from relevant cortical parcellations (e.g., superior/middle/inferior temporal for Temporal Lobe) and adjusted. Basal Forebrain data are from specialized segmentations (e.g., cholinergic BF maps integrated with FreeSurfer), which are less standard and sparser. Race is "Pooled" due to limited stratified data in open sources; most cohorts are predominantly Caucasian. Age bins start at 20-29 with 10-year intervals, up to 80-89 where data allow (some extend to 90+ but binned accordingly). 
 
Metrics Derivation: Means and SDs are directly from sources or averaged across hemispheres (left+right)/2 where reported separately. Medians are approximated as means (assuming near-normal distributions unless skewed, e.g., ventricles). Min/Max are estimated as mean ± 2SD (capped at plausible biological ranges, e.g., >0). All values are fractions (not percentages). For ROIs not fully covered in a single dataset (e.g., Basal Forebrain, full lobe aggregates), cross-study harmonization was applied (e.g., scaling to UKBB eTIV norms ~1.4-1.6 million mm³). Ventral DC (Ventral Diencephalon) and Accumbens are from aseg; Entorhinal from aparc. 
 
Limitations and Cautions: Data are from mixed FreeSurfer versions (mostly 5.3-7.0); direct v7.0-specific norms are emerging but not comprehensive for all ROIs. Temporal/Parietal lobes are approximations (sum of gray matter volumes in DK atlas regions, excluding white matter/subcorticals unless specified). Variability increases with age due to atrophy/expansion (e.g., ventricles enlarge). Not for clinical use without validation; sources may have selection biases (e.g., UKBB is healthier volunteers). Sample sizes per bin vary (e.g., 100s-1000s for UKBB). 
 
Sources Summary: Primary: UK Biobank norms for subcorticals/ventricles; Desikan-Killiany cortical norms for Entorhinal/lobes; multi-cohort papers for cross-validation and Basal Forebrain.
