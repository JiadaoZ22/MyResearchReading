Data Collection Approach: The data has been compiled from several large-scale open-access studies and databases, with a primary focus on providing normative values for brain structures as measured by FreeSurfer. The main source for subcortical and ventricular volumes is the UK Biobank, which provides data from a very large cohort of healthy adults (N > 18,000), analyzed with FreeSurfer versions 5.3 and 6.x. These norms are stratified by age and sex and are adjusted for estimated total intracranial volume (eTIV). Data for cortical regions, such as the Entorhinal Cortex and the aggregated lobes (Temporal and Parietal), are primarily derived from studies using the Desikan-Killiany atlas. Relative volumes are calculated as the ratio of the ROI volume to the eTIV. For the aggregated lobes, the volumes of the relevant cortical parcellations have been summed. The "Race" is marked as "Pooled" as the majority of the large public datasets are predominantly of Caucasian ancestry, and there is a lack of stratified normative data for other races. The age bins are structured in 10-year intervals, starting from 20-29 years.

Metrics Derivation: The mean and standard deviation (SD) values are either directly taken from the source publications or are calculated as an average of the left and right hemispheres when reported separately. In cases where the median was not explicitly provided, it has been approximated as the mean, assuming a near-normal distribution of the volumes for most regions. The minimum and maximum values are estimated as the mean ± 2 standard deviations, which represents an approximate 95% confidence interval. These values are capped at plausible biological limits (e.g., volume > 0). All volumes are presented as fractions of the total intracranial volume.

Limitations and Cautions: It is important to note that the data provided is aggregated from studies that may have used different versions of FreeSurfer (ranging from v5.3 to v7.0). While efforts have been made to harmonize the data, there may be slight inconsistencies. The volumes for the Temporal and Parietal lobes are approximations based on the sum of gray matter volumes from the Desikan-Killiany atlas parcellations and may not include all sub-regions. There is natural variability in brain volumes, which tends to increase with age, particularly for structures like the ventricles which expand as the brain atrophies. This data is for informational purposes only and should not be used for clinical diagnosis without further validation.

Sources Summary: The primary sources for this data include the neuroanatomical norms from the UK Biobank for subcortical and ventricular structures, publications on cortical normative data for regions like the Entorhinal Cortex, and multi-cohort studies for cross-validation and for ROIs like the Basal Forebrain.

Recommendations: For more precise and customized normative data, it is recommended to use tools like FreeSurfer's mri_segstats on publicly available datasets such as the Human Connectome Project (HCP) or the Alzheimer's Disease Neuroimaging Initiative (ADNI). This would allow for the creation of normative data based on specific demographics and FreeSurfer versions.
