The following data table is a synthesized approximation of normative values for relative ROI volumes (as a fraction of total intracranial volume) based on a review of open-access neuroimaging literature and databases. The values are not from a single source but represent a harmonized estimate derived from trends reported in large-scale studies (e.g., UK Biobank, ADNI, and various consortium efforts) that use FreeSurfer for volumetric analysis.

Data Origin: These values are synthesized and should be considered as placeholders or for modeling purposes. For rigorous scientific use, it is essential to refer to the primary publications and use the exact values reported therein.
Missing Data: Where "NaN" is used, it indicates that reliable, consistent data for that specific metric, age range, and sex was not readily available in the surveyed literature. Minimum and maximum values are particularly difficult to standardize across different study populations and are therefore marked as "NaN".
ROI Definitions: The ROIs are based on the standard FreeSurfer segmentation and parcellation. "Temporal Lobes" and "Parietal Lobes" are aggregate cortical volumes.
Age and Sex Trends: The data reflects general trends observed in the literature: a gradual decrease in the volume of most gray matter structures (like the Hippocampus and cortex) with age, and a corresponding increase in the volume of the Lateral Ventricles. Sex differences are generally small after correcting for intracranial volume.
Race: The "Race" is set to "All" as most large-scale normative studies are based on mixed-ancestry populations, and race-specific normative data is not yet widely available for all these ROIs.
