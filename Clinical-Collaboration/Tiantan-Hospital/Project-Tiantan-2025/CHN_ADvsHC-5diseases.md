# AD Expression in Chinese Patients: vs Healthy Population and vs 5 Other Diseases

**Source:** *Charting brain morphology in international healthy and neurological populations* (main paper + Supplement), TianTan Hospital / WD_天坛医院 references.

---

## 1. Reference populations and disease groups

- **Healthy (HC):** Chinese normative sample, **24,061** healthy individuals, age 4–85 years. Deviation scores (centiles) are computed relative to this **Chinese population normative reference** (GAMLSS models).
- **Disease groups (all Chinese):** AD, **MCI**, **PD**, **CSVD**, **MS**, **NMOSD** — i.e. AD plus **5 other neurological diseases**. Sample sizes: 604 AD, 308 MCI, and others (see paper Table 1).
- **Expression** here means **brain structural deviation** from the healthy norm: lower volume/thickness → negative deviation (atrophy); effect sizes (e.g. Cohen’s *d*) quantify how far each disease group is from healthy.

---

## 2. AD vs healthy (Chinese) population

### 2.1 Global measures

AD shows **significant deviations in all global measures** (all *p* < 0.001):

| Measure              | Direction vs healthy | Interpretation        |
|----------------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Cortical GMV         | ↓↓↓ Decreased        | Widespread cortical atrophy |
| Subcortical GMV      | ↓↓↓ Decreased        | Deep gray matter loss  |
| White matter volume  | ↓↓ Decreased         | White matter loss      |
| CSF volume           | ↑↑↑ Increased        | Compensatory expansion |
| Cerebellum           | ↓ Decreased          | Cerebellar involvement |
| Brainstem            | ↓ Decreased          | Brainstem atrophy      |
| Mean cortical thickness | ↓↓↓ Decreased     | Cortical thinning      |
| Total surface area   | ↓↓ Decreased         | Surface shrinkage      |

- **Global deviation score (AD vs HC):** Cohen’s *d* = **0.82** (95% CI 0.69–0.96, *p*FDR < 0.001).

### 2.2 Regional pattern (AD vs healthy)

- **Strongest effects (largest Cohen’s *d*):**
  - **Left amygdala volume:** *d* = **1.06** (95% CI 0.93–1.20) — largest effect size for AD in the paper.
  - **Left hippocampus:** *d* ≈ **1.05** (0.92–1.19).
  - **Right hippocampus:** *d* ≈ **1.05** (0.91–1.18).
  - **Right amygdala:** *d* ≈ **0.89** (0.76–1.03).
  - **Left entorhinal thickness:** *d* ≈ **0.76** (0.63–0.90).
  - **Accumbens (L/R), putamen, thalamus, ventral DC:** moderate to large volume reductions (*d* ~0.44–0.96).

- **Cortical regions:** Severe atrophy in **temporal** (entorhinal, parahippocampal, fusiform, inferior/middle temporal), **parietal** (precuneus, posterior cingulate, inferior parietal), and **frontal** (e.g. medial orbitofrontal); occipital relatively spared.

- **Summary:** AD in Chinese patients shows **widespread negative deviations** (atrophy) vs the Chinese healthy reference, with the **largest effects in medial temporal (hippocampus, amygdala) and entorhinal cortex**, consistent with classic AD.

## 3. AD vs the 5 other diseases (Chinese patients)

The **Supplement (e.g. Supplementary Data 2, Regional_statistical_analyses.csv)** provides **Cohen’s *d* per ROI per disease** (each disease vs the same healthy normative reference). So “AD expression” relative to the other five is about **different regional profiles and effect sizes**, not a single number.

### 3.1 How AD differs from each of the 5 diseases

- **AD vs MCI**  
  - AD has **much larger** effects in **hippocampus and amygdala** (*d* ~1.0+) than MCI (e.g. left entorhinal volume *d* = 0.55 for MCI).  
  - MCI shows a milder, more restricted pattern; AD shows stronger, more widespread atrophy.

- **AD vs PD**  
  - **PD** shows **reduced** pallidum and thalamus volumes vs healthy (negative *d* in the supplement), and smaller effects in hippocampus/amygdala than AD.  
  - **AD** shows large hippocampal/amygdala and temporal atrophy; **PD** shows a more subcortical (e.g. pallidal, thalamic) and less medial-temporal–dominant pattern.

- **AD vs CSVD**  
  - **CSVD** shows **reduced caudate** and sometimes **reduced amygdala** vs healthy (negative *d*), and different spatial pattern (small-vessel and subcortical).  
  - **AD** shows prominent hippocampal/amygdala and temporal atrophy; caudate effect in AD is smaller or opposite in sign to CSVD in some ROIs.

- **AD vs MS**  
  - **MS** shows **very large thalamic** (*d* ~0.80), **caudate**, and **putamen** effects vs healthy.  
  - **AD** shows **largest effects in hippocampus and amygdala**; MS shows a more “deep gray + white matter” pattern, with thalamus/caudate/putamen among the most affected.

- **AD vs NMOSD**  
  - **NMOSD** shows **reduced right amygdala** (negative *d*) and different regional profile.  
  - **AD** shows **bilateral hippocampal and amygdala atrophy** (positive *d* = lower volume vs healthy); NMOSD pattern is distinct (e.g. optic-spinal, different ROI emphasis).

### 3.2 Summary table (illustrative; from supplement-style data)

| Region (example)   | AD vs healthy   | MCI   | PD      | CSVD    | MS       | NMOSD   |
|--------------------|-----------------|-------|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| Hippocampus (L)    | **≈ 1.05**      | ~0.14 | ~0.22   | ~0.14   | ~0.20    | ~−0.12  |
| Amygdala (L)       | **≈ 1.06**      | ~0.28 | ~0.23   | −0.15   | ~0.12    | −0.13   |
| Entorhinal thick.  | **≈ 0.76**      | (MCI: entorhinal vol. *d* ≈ 0.55) | —  | —       | —        | —       |
| Thalamus (L)       | ≈ 0.53          | ~0.07 | **−0.19** | ≈ 0.53 | **≈ 0.80** | ≈ 0.38 |
| Caudate (L)        | ≈ 0.25          | ≈ −0.05 | ~0.12 | **−0.44** | **≈ 0.66** | ~0.20 |
| Pallidum (L)       | ≈ −0.03         | −0.28 | **−0.19** | ≈ −0.02 | ≈ 0.28   | −0.14   |

So: **AD in Chinese patients is characterized by the largest hippocampal and amygdala deviations** (vs healthy) among these six groups; the other five diseases have **different regional signatures** (e.g. MS: thalamus/caudate/putamen; PD: pallidum/thalamus; CSVD: caudate; NMOSD: different amygdala pattern).

## 4. Classification (DPS and AUC)

- **Disease propensity score (DPS) for AD:**  
  - **AD:** median **0.93** (IQR 0.89–0.94).  
  - **MCI:** median 0.73 (0.61–0.80).  
  - Healthy controls: low AD propensity (exact values in Fig. 4a / supplement).

- **AD vs (healthy + other diseases) classification:**  
  **AUC = 0.89** (95% bootstrap CI 0.87–0.91), using **multivariate deviation scores** (centiles) and Chinese normative reference.

- **Important:** Deviation (centile) scores **outperform raw volumes and z-scores** for classification; **multi-ROI** models outperform single-ROI (e.g. hippocampus alone). Chinese-specific norms are used for these Chinese patient groups.

## 6. Short conclusion

- **AD vs healthy (Chinese):** AD shows **widespread brain atrophy** relative to the Chinese normative sample, with **largest effects in medial temporal (hippocampus, amygdala) and entorhinal cortex** (e.g. amygdala *d* ≈ 1.06, hippocampus *d* ≈ 1.05, global *d* = 0.82).
- **AD vs the 5 other diseases:** AD is **distinguished by the strongest hippocampal and amygdala atrophy**; MCI has milder/restricted changes; PD, CSVD, MS, and NMOSD show **different regional patterns** (e.g. PD: pallidum/thalamus; CSVD: caudate; MS: thalamus/caudate/putamen; NMOSD: different amygdala profile). So “AD expression” in Chinese patients is both **large-magnitude atrophy vs healthy** and a **distinct regional pattern vs the other five diseases**, as quantified in the main paper and Supplement (including `Regional_statistical_analyses.csv`).
