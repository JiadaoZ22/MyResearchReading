The following table provides approximate normative relative volumes (structure volume / total intracranial volume, ICV) for key FreeSurfer ROIs across adulthood (age bands 20–89, 10-year bins) separated by sex. These synthesized values are derived by harmonizing ranges reported across large open neuroimaging cohorts and methodological papers (e.g., UK Biobank, multi-cohort ENIGMA-derived summaries, ADNI-related publications) and typical FreeSurfer aseg/aparc outputs. They are NOT a verbatim extraction of a single source; instead they represent a pragmatic integrated approximation meant for placeholder or modeling use. For formal analyses, you should replace them with values directly extracted from the specific datasets/publications you rely on.
Hippocampus and entorhinal cortex show gradual age-related decline after ~40; lateral ventricles expand with age; nucleus accumbens and ventral diencephalon show comparatively small fractional change; cortical lobar fractions (temporal, parietal) exhibit mild monotonic decline; basal forebrain (Ch1–4 composite) shows subtle decline.
Sex differences after ICV normalization are small; females often show slightly higher relative limbic fractions (hippocampus, amygdala) consistent with several normalization studies, while males show slightly higher ventricular fraction in younger and older groups.
Standard deviations provided reflect typical inter-individual variability after basic QC in healthy populations (no neurological/psychiatric diagnosis) and assume consistent FreeSurfer processing (v6–v7 family). They are intentionally conservative (i.e., may be slightly wider than very large harmonized consortia outputs) to avoid overprecision.
Median is set equal to mean here (distributions near-symmetric in healthy cohorts after ICV normalization). Min and Max are approximated as mean ± 2*SD (bounded below by >0). If you need empirical quantiles (e.g., 5th/95th), recompute from raw data.
ROIs match standard FreeSurfer labels (aseg + cortical parcellations). Temporal/Parietal Lobes here refer to combined bilateral cortical gray + adjacent white matter compartments (aggregate); for high-fidelity work you may wish to separate GM/WM or use surface-based thickness/area instead of volumetric fraction.
