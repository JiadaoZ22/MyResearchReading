Scope: The table below provides provisional relative volume fractions (ROI volume / total intracranial volume (TIV)) for nine FreeSurfer-defined (or commonly derived) regions across adult age bins (10‑year bins starting at 20) and by sex. Race is set to Pooled because stratified normative data across all requested ROIs are sparse in openly accessible large datasets.
Sources used: Large population FreeSurfer normative resources (UK Biobank neuroanatomical norms), hippocampal nomograms, multi-cohort normative volumetry comparisons, cortical atlas normative data, and basal forebrain segmentation studies. These sources report absolute volumes (and sometimes ICV-adjusted metrics); relative fractions here are derived by dividing published (or typical) mean absolute volumes by representative mean TIV values (approx male 1,550,000 mm3; female 1,400,000 mm3) and then applying literature-consistent age trajectories (atrophy or expansion patterns). 
Important caution: Many entries (especially Basal Forebrain, aggregated Temporal and Parietal lobes as whole-lobe composites, and Ventral Diencephalon) are synthesized estimates rather than direct, uniformly extracted values from a single harmonized dataset. They serve as placeholders to illustrate structure and approximate magnitudes. Do NOT use clinically or for rigorous statistical benchmarking without replacing with directly extracted values.
Variation estimates: Standard deviations are approximated as a proportion of the mean (e.g., Hippocampus ~7–9%, Amygdala ~8–10%, Lateral Ventricles larger ~18–25%) informed by dispersion reported in large-cohort publications. Min and max are set to mean ±2 SD (floored at >0). Median assumed ≈ mean for symmetric distributions.
Next steps recommended: (a) Direct extraction from a consistent FreeSurfer version (preferably v7.x) across a large dataset (e.g., UK Biobank) using native aseg + lobar parcellations; (b) Use generalized additive models (GAM) to derive smooth age trends and then re-bin; (c) Provide sex- and ICV-adjusted z-scores; (d) For Basal Forebrain, apply a validated probabilistic atlas (e.g., cholinergic basal forebrain map) for consistent segmentation.
Replacement workflow (summary): Acquire absolute volumes -> compute TIV (eTIV) -> aggregate by AgeBin/Sex -> compute fraction, mean, SD, median, empirical min/max -> export to identical CSV schema for reproducibility.
